You have hard time in hiring that one developer ?

Lot of folks have approached me personally to recommend developer for their startup.

What have you tried ?

You posted job description in best job board in your country, still no response ? It happens. There are several reasons.

What you should try ?

  1. Do you open source any of your company code? Don’t say me we are building next X which can’t be open sourced. This is complete bull shit. You will be writing large application which can be assembled as small piece which have high chance of open sourcing. By open sourcing your work, you attract like minded people. You get free pull request, patches.

  2. Do you answer questions in mailing list, quora, stackoverflow? By answering you get to be noticed by folks in mailing list and other like minded people, some one in the list may be that developer you are looking for.

  3. Do you attend meetups ? By attending/talking at meetup you are likely to meet hackers who may be looking for job.

  4. List your startup’s open source work, employees in the company site.

when I was looking for the job, this is what I did.

  1. Look into that particular company code base in github, if they don’t have github repo, move on.
  2. Look into the members of organization and their code base, questions, replies in stackoverflow, quora, reddit.

Remember “Talk is cheap, show me the code” — Linus Torvalds.

Kickstarter project for social cause, which I am supporting

I viewed kickstarter as a place to expose tech savy ideas like stompy for crowd funding. Today I came across the project the-vanni, a multi media, interactive comic book about conflict and migration focusing on Sri Lanka.

There is a documentary released by channel4 which provides complete information about unjustice faced by civilans(Tamil’s) during 2009 war between LTTE & Sri Lankan Government.

Why I am supporting this project ?
  1. I am a Tamilan.
  2. Attempt to seek justice for Tamil Eelam natives of northern eastern Sri Lanka.
  3. Benjamin Dix was based in the Vanni from 2004 until the UN evacuation in 2008.
Who else is supporting?
  • Arundhati Roy (Booker Prize Winner, God of Small Things)
  • Jon Snow (Newscaster, Channel 4)
  • Frances Harrison (BBC and Author, Still Counting the Dead)
  • Callum Macrae (Film Maker, Sri Lanka’s Killing Fields)
  • Vairamuttu Varadakumar (Executive Secretary, Tamil Information Centre)
  • Liv Torres (Secretary General, Norwegian People’s Aid)
  • Roma Tearne (Author, The Road to Urbino)
  • Beate Arnestad (Filmmaker, My Daughter the Terrorist)

Please pledge if you wish and spread the word about the project in social media. Offical Website

2 weeks after installing Ubuntu 12.04 64bit in MacBook

Its been two weeks, since I installed Ubuntu 64 bit in MacBook. Let me say how I feel about.

My setup isn’t complicated, I use sublime2, terminator, gnome-terminal, ipython, pypy-2.0beta, postgres, mosh, firefox-nightly, chromium, chrome, clementine, xchat-gnome, hotot(recent addition).

Also installed nividia drivers for ubuntu. I am happy I installed ubuntu but had issue with right click and figured shift + F10 is short cut.

Hate Hate Hate

If you are using data-card you must have noticed default modem-manager is buggy. If I suspend my system for 10 hours and try to activate the device no response. You kill the process and restart it doesn’t work, So I need to restart the machine. Really din’t find solution for it :-(.

Occassionally after resuming from suspend mode, system load is 100%, I don’t know reason is due to firefox nightly or hotot(I had lot memory leaks in past), only way to find out memory usage of firefox is about:memory which doesn’t display tab wise memory usage rather by componenet wise.

I like Mac keyboard and weight compared to my toshibha not sure my next laptop will be MacBook.
